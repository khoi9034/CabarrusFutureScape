# CFS What Not To Claim

Use this sheet to keep demos accurate, confident, and honest.

## Do Not Claim

- CFS predicts exact parcel development probability.
- CFS is production-ready.
- CFS makes entitlement or permitting decisions.
- CFS confirms utility capacity or allocation.
- CFS has official school capacity scoring.
- CFS has official school overcrowding findings.
- CFS has complete official rezoning case history.
- CFS is a public decision engine.
- CFS replaces staff planning, engineering, floodplain, school, or utility
  review.
- CFS proves causality between a feature and future development.
- CFS exposes parcel-level prediction probabilities.
- CFS exposes parcel-level model ranking classes.
- Model Lab is an official production prediction product.

## Approved Wording

- "Internal planning intelligence prototype."
- "Parcel-based due diligence workflow."
- "Evidence review and constraint screening."
- "Model research is aggregate-only and not public-facing."
- "Current best internal model research is Zoning + Transportation + Tax/Value."
- "Model Lab is an internal research preview, not a public parcel score."
- "Development Research Signal shows relative research bands, not exact
  probability."
- "Driver explanations show why a research marker was highlighted; they are
  not entitlement findings."
- "Utility layers are proxy/context only."
- "School utilization seed requires verification."
- "Official school capacity scoring is not active."
- "FEMA NFHL is the authoritative regulatory flood source used for flood
  context."
- "Planning decisions remain human-reviewed."

## Model Safety Wording

Use:

- "internal research only";
- "not production-ready";
- "calibration review required";
- "no exact probabilities are shown";
- "no public parcel prediction endpoint exists".

Avoid:

- "forecast";
- "this parcel will develop";
- "development risk score";
- "final prediction";
- "automated recommendation".
- "official model class".

## School Wording

Use:

- "attendance-zone assignment";
- "presentation-derived utilization";
- "needs verification";
- "capacity data needed";
- "not scored".

Avoid:

- "official overcrowding";
- "confirmed school capacity pressure";
- "student impact forecast".

## Utility Wording

Use:

- "utility proxy context";
- "service context";
- "official data needed";
- "capacity not confirmed".

Avoid:

- "capacity available";
- "sewer allocation confirmed";
- "utility approval likely".
