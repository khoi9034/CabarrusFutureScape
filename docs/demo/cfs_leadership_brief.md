# CFS Leadership Brief

## What CFS Is

Cabarrus FutureScape is an internal planning intelligence prototype for
parcel-level due diligence, growth review, and constraint visibility. It brings
parcel records, zoning context, permit activity, flood constraints, school
assignment, transportation access, utility proxy context, and model governance
into one local review workspace.

CFS is designed to help staff and leaders answer a practical question:

"What do we know about this parcel, what constraints matter, and what should
staff review next?"

## Why It Matters

Planning review often requires staff to check several systems, GIS layers,
spreadsheets, and public records before they can explain parcel context. CFS
reduces that friction by organizing the evidence around the parcel.

The prototype supports:

- faster parcel due diligence;
- clearer communication during map-based review;
- repeatable review language for constraints and caveats;
- transparent separation between verified data, presentation-derived data,
  proxy context, and internal model research.

## Data Brought Together

CFS currently integrates or inventories:

- parcel and assessor-derived parcel context;
- zoning and historical zoning source context;
- development activity and permit intelligence;
- new construction permit history for internal model research;
- FEMA NFHL flood constraints and FEMA Flood Zones;
- public CCS school attendance-zone assignments;
- presentation-derived SY 2024-2025 school utilization seed values;
- transportation accessibility, STIP, and AADT context;
- utility proxy context from available GIS layers;
- internal model QA, governance, and readiness metadata.

## What Staff Can Use It For Today

Today, CFS can support:

- parcel search and selected-parcel review;
- high-level constraint screening;
- flood review visibility;
- school assignment caveat review;
- permit and development history review;
- transportation and utility context review;
- stakeholder-friendly Executive Print report previews;
- methodology and data limitation discussion.

CFS is best described today as an internal planning intelligence and due
diligence prototype.

## What The Model Does and Does Not Do

CFS includes internal development model research using new construction permit
outcomes and parcel/context features. The current best internal research
variant is:

- Zoning + Transportation + Tax/Value

This model research is not production-ready and is not public-facing.

It does:

- support internal model QA and feature governance;
- compare feature groups using aggregate metrics;
- document calibration and safety limitations.

It does not:

- show exact parcel-level probabilities;
- show public parcel-level ranking classes;
- make parcel development forecasts for public decisions;
- replace staff review or entitlement processes.

## What Is Intentionally Not Exposed

CFS intentionally does not expose:

- parcel-level prediction probabilities;
- parcel-level development ranking classes;
- public prediction endpoints;
- production-ready model wording;
- official school capacity scores;
- confirmed utility capacity or allocation;
- final decision recommendations.

## Data Still Needed

The next high-value data requests are:

- official school enrollment and functional capacity files;
- official rezoning case records with dates, old zoning, and new zoning;
- future land use and small area plan data with clear adoption dates;
- utility service capacity and allocation information;
- local transportation project data with status and expected year;
- subdivision/development pipeline records with dates and geography.

## Recommended Next Investments

1. Formalize official data-sharing paths for school, utility, planning, and
   transportation inputs.
2. Continue model governance before any public-facing prediction surface.
3. Harden Executive Print into a production report/export workflow.
4. Add staff review workflows for comments, issue tracking, and exportable
   findings.
5. Prepare deployment architecture only after data governance and security
   expectations are clear.
